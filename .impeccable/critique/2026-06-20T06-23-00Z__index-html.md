## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does it look AI-generated? No — and convincingly so.**

**LLM assessment**: This passes the slop test on purpose. It uses real brand assets (the official ZIBELLO/ARENA SVG pulled from the PDF, the bonfire emblem), a committed navy-night palette tied to a real fact (matches are played in the evening), a genuine display typeface (Sailors) with scoreboard-style numerals, and a bottom tab bar. It dodges the usual tells: no cream/editorial bg, no gradient text, no per-section uppercase eyebrows, no hero-metric template (the stats grid was deliberately removed), no glassmorphism-by-default. Category-reflex check passes at both altitudes: the look isn't guessable from "bocce tournament," and the night/bonfire identity is this brand's own, not a generic anti-reflex lane.

**Deterministic scan**: `detect.mjs --json index.html` → `[]` (exit 0, clean). No markup-level anti-patterns. (Caveat: the detector scans markup only; the CSS-driven decisions were judged visually in Assessment A.)

**Visual overlays**: Not injected — with zero detector findings the overlay would highlight nothing. Browser evidence came from full-page screenshots of all four views at desktop (1310px) and mobile (375px).

## Overall Impression

This is a genuinely good, purpose-built microsite that nails its primary job — *"find my match, see who's winning"* — on a phone, with real identity instead of template gloss. The home, calendario and classifiche views are confident and clean. The single biggest opportunity isn't visual polish; it's **closing the loop for the repeat participant**: the person who opens this every match night to check their own team should land on *their* team, not re-filter each time. The secondary gap is **orientation**: removing the Info page left newcomers with no place to learn the format or what "V/G" means.

## What's Working

1. **Real brand, not AI gloss.** The official vector wordmark, the glowing bonfire, the navy-night theme, Sailors numerals on scores — these read as *someone's* tournament, not a generated one. This is the hardest thing to get right and it's right.
2. **Mobile-first mechanics.** Bottom tab bar (thumb zone), calendar auto-scrolls to today, sticky filterbar that correctly stacks under the header, responsive tables that restack cleanly. The core flow is fast on a phone.
3. **Data clarity and integrity.** Standings recomputed from results (Vinte→scontro diretto→Differenza), girone color-coding as a fast index across 32 teams, winners in bold, played-vs-upcoming distinguished by score-vs-time.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] No "my team" — the repeat participant re-filters every visit.** The stated #1 job is "quando gioco / com'è andata la mia squadra," yet the team filter resets each load and nothing surfaces a single team's next match + last result.
  - *Why it matters*: The most frequent action (a player checking their own team nightly) is also the least supported; they repeat the same filter every time.
  - *Fix*: Persist the last team filter in `localStorage`; better, add a "La mia squadra" picker that pins that team's next fixture + latest result to the top of Home (and pre-selects it in the calendar).
  - *Suggested command*: `/impeccable harden` (persistence + edge cases), or `/impeccable craft` for the pinned-team feature.

- **[P1] Orientation gap after removing Info.** There's no central place that explains the format (32→gironi→ottavi→finale), how qualification works, or how to read the tables.
  - *Why it matters*: First-timers and family/spectators can't decode the standings or the empty bracket; the knowledge lives only in the author's head.
  - *Fix*: A lightweight "Come funziona" affordance — a short expandable on Classifiche/Tabellone, or a slim about strip in the footer — covering format, qualification, and the V/G/Diff legend. Doesn't need to be a full page.
  - *Suggested command*: `/impeccable onboard` (teach the structure) or `/impeccable clarify` (legend + copy).

- **[P2] Tabellone empty state is barren and uninformative.** 16 ties × "Da definire" / "Da definire" (32 placeholders), horizontal-scroll on mobile, with no qualification mapping and no scroll affordance.
  - *Why it matters*: Navigating to a full nav section and finding only repeated placeholders is an emotional valley; it currently teaches nothing.
  - *Fix*: Fill the slots with seeding labels ("1ª Girone A", "2ª Girone H", "Vincente Ottavo 1"…) so the structure is legible before teams are known; add a left/right scroll hint; consider a clearer empty-state line.
  - *Suggested command*: `/impeccable onboard` or `/impeccable layout`.

- **[P2] "V/G" is unclear on mobile.** The abbreviation is explained only via a `title` tooltip (hover = desktop-only); touch and screen-reader users get nothing.
  - *Why it matters*: The standings are a primary surface; an unexplained column header on the dominant (mobile) platform is a clarity miss.
  - *Fix*: Use `<abbr>` with an accessible expansion and/or a small visible legend ("V/G = Vinte / Giocate · Diff = Differenza punti") under the standings.
  - *Suggested command*: `/impeccable clarify`.

- **[P3] Girone filter: touch + density.** The 9 chips (Tutti + A–H) sit in a ~34px-tall scroll row — under the 44px touch target, and a 9-option row is a mild "wall of options."
  - *Why it matters*: Casey (one-handed, on the go) mis-taps small adjacent chips.
  - *Fix*: Bump chip height to ≥40px on mobile and add a subtle scroll affordance.
  - *Suggested command*: `/impeccable adapt`.

## Persona Red Flags

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User)** — primary persona here:
- Team filter NOT persisted: re-picks their team on every visit (worst offender for the core use case).
- Girone filter chips ~34px tall → below the 44px thumb target; adjacent chips invite mis-taps.
- Good: bottom nav in the thumb zone; calendar opens at today; state survives within a session via the hash.

**Marco, il giocatore (project persona — checks his own team match night)**:
- Has to mentally scan 9 gironi / 48 matches or filter manually to find his fixture; no "my team" shortcut.
- Can't tell at a glance whether tonight's results are already entered (no freshness signal since "aggiornato il…" was removed).

**Jordan (First-Timer / family, never seen it)**:
- "V/G" unexplained on mobile; no "how it works"; the all-"Da definire" bracket reads as broken rather than not-yet-drawn.
- Good: nav labels are text, the hero tagline says what the site is, the Maps link answers "where."

**Sam (Accessibility)**:
- Mostly well served: contrast verified (body ≥8:1, chips ≥5:1), `:focus-visible` rings, reduced-motion handled, winners shown by weight not color alone, `<th scope>` on tables, SVG icons `aria-hidden`, nav has real labels.
- Gap: "V/G" `title` isn't reliably announced; expose it via `<abbr>`/`aria-label`.

## Minor Observations

- Section title/note baseline alignment: fixed and clean now.
- Mandated girone colors (pure `#00FF00`, etc.) are loud against the refined navy/red/white, but confined to small chips — an acceptable constraint, not a failure.
- Bracket reads as four columns; real connector lines would make the progression clearer once populated.
- Home "Ultimi risultati" + "Prossime partite" (next matchday only) are well-judged for the primary job.
- No data-freshness indicator anywhere (deliberately removed); for a *results* site this is a real, if intentional, tradeoff.
